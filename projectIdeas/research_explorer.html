The unifying thread

"Where does grounding die in the multimodal stack?"

Four of the buckets below are secretly the same phenomenon — visual/grounding information exists inside the model but one modality dominates and it gets lost at a specific pipeline stage:

  • Counting → spatial signal drifts to text priors at the LLM stage ("visual attention sink")
  • 3D scenes → structure recoverable in the ViT & first LM layer, then destroyed at the visual-token merger
  • VLAs → language ignored; vision priors dominate ("instruction blindness")
  • Compression → makes the loss worse, invisibly (aggregate accuracy hides it)

A project playbook that spans all of them: find where grounding dies → prove it → poke it. Diagnostic/mechanistic, which builds the "why does it break" muscle.
